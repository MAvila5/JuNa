JuNá's Bio

JuNá is a senior chemistry student at the University of Central Florida.

Her research is centered in chemistry education under the direction of Dr. Christopher Randles with a focus on sociocultural literacy. Her current research study is trying to better understand how historically marginalized populations such as African Americans, Asian Americans, Hispanics or Latinx, Native Americans, and women face unique challenges both in applying to and attending college.

The study aims to identify and present replicable practice(s) that can be employed to further the diversity of STEM majors with a focus in chemistry. Isomerism of Glycine complexes

10.02.2020

In this experiment, cis and trans isomers of copper (II) glycinate are prepared and characterized by IR spectroscopy. The direct reaction of copper(II) acetate monohydrate and glycine results in an equilibrium mixture of the two isomers. Copper (II) glycinate gives rise to a square planar configuration of the oxygen and nitrogen atoms around the copper leading to two geometrically different ways of positioning the glycines. Compounds that have the same formula but different structures are called isomers and the special type of isomerism present here is called geometric isomerism. With different configurations comes different molecular symmetry; because the isomers have different point group symmetries, they can be distinguished on the basis of their vibrational spectra . The cis isomer of copper (II) glycinate is a C2v point group, while on the other hand the trans isomer of copper (II) glycinate is a C2h point group.

Ruthenium is complexed with three chelated ligands forming an octahedral configuration (figure 1). The bipyridine ligands have empty π orbitals that can coordinate with the six electrons in the metals dπb orbital subshell and bond by donation of the lone pairs on the nitrogen.The complex [Ru(bpy)3]2+ emits orange light in solution. This low-spin d6 complex absorbs strongly in the visible region of the spectrum due to MLCT transitions, which are also the lowest energy excited state. The lowest energy emitting state is best described as a triplet (3 MLCT) excited state. This state occurs when an electron is promoted from the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) to an empty ligand π* orbital, the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO).
